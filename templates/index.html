TERM DEPOSITS

What is Term Deposit?

Term Deposits are generally low risk investment options available to the general consumers in particular. It enables the investor to earn monthly, quarterly; half yearly; yearly or profit at maturity.

For the term you choose, you are locked in, it can be anything from one month to 5 years. Conventional banks give you the profit you are locked in for the whole period. While Islamic Banks profit varies depending on profit and its distribution. Generally Islamic Banks offer lower rates than Conventional Banks and National Savings.

How to find the best options?

  • Rates vary from one Bank/ financier to another based on their policy.
  • Nowadays, most of the banks are offering variable rate.
  • Make sure there is initial investment charges, Mutual Funds have sales loading charges, if the return justifies then go ahead.
  • However, if invest for shorter duration even for a year you would not be able to cover the loading charges ranges between 1-3% of the invested amount.
  • Look at premature redemption charges if any.
  • How much time they take in redemption, service quality.

How & What to Compare:

Our comparison sheet will give a good understanding, what you need to look at is :

  • Rate
  • Tenor/ Length of period- Maturity
  • Repayment Frequency
  • Minimum investment Required
  • Bank/ Financial Institution Rating
  • Rollover Facility

Pros & Cons of Term Deposits:
Pros Cons
It is a low risk investment compared to other options. Low risk thus it has low return especially on long term.
Profit rates are fixed giving a sense of income stream. Except Banking where profit rates are variable and generally lower than conventional banking. Rates are not attractive if you invest for long term.
No upfront charges for investment. Unlike Mutual Funds
Easily encashable However, premature encashment penalty will be applied. Applicable rate will be applied for the period money remain invested. The difference will be recovered if any.

Inflation & Term Deposit Rates:

Normally Term Deposit Rate(TDR) of return is lower than the rate of inflation. If that is case then customers are not earning enough to match the price increase in the economy. Say if the TDR is 18% and the inflation is 38%, then it is not earning enough to meet the rising prices of goods, hence your moneys capacity to purchase is gone down.

Thus it would be a good idea to go for shorter duration of investment where rates are better and may change, so that you can take advantage of.

National Savings

National Savings is a state owned savings Bank of Pakistan. It operates as an attached department of Finance Division, Ministry of Finance. Thus it has an edge over others in terms of Risk, that is negligible.

National Savings offers, 3 months to 10 years products of monthly profit and profit at maturity. Retired individuals generally go for monthly profit to meet their daily expenses while younger lot go for long term investment taking principal and profit at maturity when the need arise or it is reinvestment for certain period.

Use the calculator to know the monthly profit/ profit at maturity.

Look at how much you can get if you invest 10 lac for 10 years maturity. The Present profit rate of Defense Savings Certificate is 14.87%

After 10 years, you would get Rs. 33.19175 lac In the shape of Principal + Profit.

Regular Income Certificates

Similarly I f you decide to invest in Regular Income Certificate for 5 years maturity, and monthly profit with current rate of profit is 14.28%.

Again if invest Rs.10 lac for 5 years, you would get Rs. 23,414 monthly profit

Behbood Savings Certificates

In the same manner if you decide to invest in Behbood Savings Certificate of 10 years maturity with Monthly profit rate of 16.56%, you would get Rs.24.617/- in monthly profit.

Please note that only Behbood Savings Certificate are exempt from taxes & zakat.

Special Savings Certificates

Special Savings Certificates are offered for 3 years max. Its a medium term investment, in which profits are paid 6 monthly and offers an attractive rate of 18.20% as of July 12,2023.

Taxes and Zakat are applicable.
